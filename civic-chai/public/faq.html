Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about Civic Chai

General Questions

What is Civic Chai? +
Civic Chai is a civic incident management platform that enables citizens to report public issues quickly while automatically prioritizing them based on real-world criticality. The system enforces time-bound resolution through SLAs, escalation, and proof-based closure, ensuring transparency and accountability in public service delivery.
How is Civic Chai different from other complaint platforms? +
Unlike other platforms, Civic Chai: (1) Automatically prioritizes issues by criticality, (2) Enforces time-bound SLAs with auto-escalation, (3) Requires photo proof of resolution, (4) Allows citizen verification of fixes, and (5) Tracks performance metrics instead of subjective star ratings. We focus on action, not just complaints.
Is Civic Chai free to use? +
Yes! Civic Chai is completely free for citizens. Our mission is to improve public service delivery and accountability, making it accessible to everyone.

Reporting Issues

How do I report an issue? +
Simply click "Report Issue" on our website or scan a QR code if available. Select the issue type, enter the location, provide a brief description, and optionally upload a photo. The entire process takes under 30 seconds.
Do I need to create an account? +
No account is required to report issues. You'll receive an Issue ID to track your report. However, providing your contact number (optional) allows us to send you status updates.
What types of issues can I report? +
You can report any public civic issue: sewage/drainage problems, broken streetlights, potholes, public toilet issues, electrical hazards, damaged public property, graffiti, overgrown vegetation, and more. If it affects public spaces or services, you can report it.
Should I upload a photo? +
While optional, photos are highly recommended. They help authorities verify the issue, assess its severity accurately, and prioritize faster. Clear photos also serve as evidence of the problem's condition.

What are the response times for each priority? +
Critical issues: Within hours | High priority: Within 1 day | Medium priority: Within few days | Low priority: Within a week. These are SLA commitments, and if not met, the issue automatically escalates to senior officials.
Can I dispute the priority assigned to my issue? +
Priority is determined automatically based on objective criteria to ensure fairness. However, if you believe there are exceptional circumstances, you can provide additional details in the description or contact us with your Issue ID.

Tracking & Resolution

How do I track my reported issue? +
Use your Issue ID (provided when you submit the report) on our Track Status page. You'll see real-time updates on your issue's status, priority level, and timeline.
What happens if authorities don't respond in time? +
If an issue isn't addressed within its SLA deadline, it automatically escalates to senior officials. Citizens can see this escalation happening in real-time. This ensures no issue gets forgotten.
How do I know the issue is actually fixed? +
Authorities must upload after-photos and describe the action taken to close an issue. As the reporter, you can review this evidence and either accept the resolution or reject it if the problem isn't actually fixed. Rejected issues automatically reopen.
What if the same issue happens again? +
Report it again! Civic Chai tracks repeated failures at the same location. This data helps identify systemic problems that need permanent solutions rather than temporary fixes.

Privacy & Data

What information is made public? +
Only the issue details (type, location, description, photo) and resolution status are public. Your personal contact information is never shared publicly. Performance metrics are aggregated and anonymized.
How is my data protected? +
We use industry-standard encryption and security practices. Your contact information is stored securely and used only for issue updates. We never sell or share your personal data with third parties.
